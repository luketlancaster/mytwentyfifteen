Jane Says

This is a wretched beginning indeed! My sole dependence was on you; and I am sure nobody else will believe me, if you do not. Well, then--supposing them to be in London. I certainly have not the talent which some people possess of conversing easily with those I have never seen before. And have you heard from him often? I often tell my other girls they are nothing to _her_. I am astonished that my father should have left so small a collection of books. The adieu is charity itself. While I can have my mornings to myself it is enough--I think it is no sacrifice to join occasionally in evening engagements. I cannot think of it without abhorrence.
  • December 16, 1775
